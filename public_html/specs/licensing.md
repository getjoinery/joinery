# Joinery Licensing

## Tiers

### Personal / Noncommercial — Free
**License:** PolyForm Noncommercial License 1.0.0

Anyone may use Joinery for personal or noncommercial purposes at no cost.

### Commercial — Paid
**License:** Elastic License 2.0 (ELv2)

Businesses may use Joinery for any internal purpose. Key restrictions:

- Cannot offer Joinery as a hosted or managed service to third parties
- Cannot move, change, disable, or circumvent license key functionality
- Cannot remove or obscure licensing, copyright, or other notices

## Rationale

**Why ELv2 for commercial?**  
ELv2 is short (~200 words), battle-tested, and widely understood. Its core restriction — no offering the software as a hosted/managed service — directly covers the main concern (someone hosting Joinery as a competing SaaS). The non-sublicensable, non-transferable grant covers reselling without needing an explicit clause. No custom drafting required.

**Why keep the license key clause?**  
Joinery does not currently have license key gating, but the clause is harmless and leaves room to add it later without updating the license.

**Why no non-compete clause?**  
A standard business licensee is already prohibited from distributing or sublicensing the software, so building a competing product using the licensed code is already blocked. A separate non-compete clause adds complexity and can deter legitimate customers without providing meaningful additional protection.

**Why PolyForm Noncommercial for personal?**  
Clean, purpose-built license for exactly this use case. Well understood, no ambiguity.

**Why no second commercial tier?**  
Larger customers who need white-labeling, agency deployment, or redistribution rights would negotiate a custom agreement. No need for a public second-tier license.
